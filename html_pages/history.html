History

Wood as a material for the manufacture of products has a number of advantages that none of the structural materials pos- sesses. Wood has a low volume weight with relatively high strength; pine, larch, fir wood for each gram of its weight withstands the same tensile load as steel, 3 times great- er than cast aluminum and 7 times greater than cast iron. The thermal conductivity of wood is 2-4 times less than the thermal conductivity of glass, 4-9 times
less than the thermal conduc- tivity of reinforced concrete and hundreds of times less than the thermal conductivity of steel. Wood as a raw material is used to produce many types of products. These are furniture (cabinet, lattice, upholstered), joinery and construction prod- ucts (windows, doors, parquet, milled parts), musical instru- ments (pianos, pianos, violins, guitars, cellos), wooden vessels (boats, yachts, canoes, rowing vessels), sports equipment (skis,
clubs, towns, bits), containers (boxes, barrels, bottles), etc. Wood is an excellent materi- al for the internal equipment of passenger railway cars and motor ships, parts and assem- blies of agricultural machinery trucks, cases of radios and tele- visions, watches, microscopes and precision mechanics devic- es, caskets, dishes, art products are made from it, sawn products are made from wood, as well as plywood, joinery and chip- boards, it is used in the con- struction of bridges, marinas, it is indispensable in the produc- tion of shoe pads, tool handles, coils, shuttles, matches, pencils.
